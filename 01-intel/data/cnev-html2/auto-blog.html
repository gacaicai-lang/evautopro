Complete Guide to Exporting Vehicles from China to Saudi Arabia: Customs Clearance, Documentation, and Best Practices

Home / Knowledge Center / Complete Guide to Exporting Vehicles from China to Saudi Arabia: Customs Clearance, Documentation, and Best Practices

While Saudi Arabia is boosting efforts to transfer to electric cars and open its market to the world’s automakers, the export of Chinese cars to the Gulf region has become increasingly brisk. In view of strong demand for fuel-efficient and new energy vehicles (NEVs), international dealers, importers and fleet buyers are now seeking out China’s automotive supply chain. But one crucial point to note is that in order to export vehicles to Saudi Arabia, there must be careful planning, adherence to the regulations, and full knowledge of the customs clearance process. This article will guide you through the entire process of importing cars from China to Saudi Arabia: all the required documentation, certifications, shipping methods, taxes and risk management.

1. Pre-Export Preparation in China

Compliance and Qualification

To export vehicles to Saudi Arabia, the Chinese exporter must meet the following regulatory requirements:

  • Import-Export License: The exporting entity in China must hold a valid import-export license.

  • SASO Certification: Vehicles must comply with Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organization (SASO) regulations. This includes structural safety, emissions, lighting, and labeling standards.

  • Energy Efficiency Certificate (for EVs and hybrids): New energy vehicles require GSO energy efficiency certification, which should be initiated at least 2–3 months in advance due to longer approval lead times.

Required Export Documents

All documentation must be accurately prepared in both English and Chinese (Arabic translation may be required later):

  • Commercial Invoice

  • Packing List

  • Sales Contract

  • Export License or CIQ Certificate (for certain vehicle categories)

  • Certificate of Origin (COO) — necessary for duty calculation

  • SASO Certificate of Conformity (SASO-COC) — mandatory for Saudi customs clearance

Important Note: The vehicle’s VIN, engine number, and specifications must match exactly with the documents. Saudi Customs enforcement is strict and mismatches can lead to cargo holds or fines.

Domestic Customs Declaration

The export process begins with:

  • Customs Declaration Filing

  • Customs Inspection

  • Release for Shipment

  • Loading and Sealing the Container

If eligible, don’t forget to apply for export VAT rebates (usually 9%–13%) after customs release, using the customs declaration and invoice.

2. International Shipping to Saudi Arabia

Recommended: Full Container Load (FCL)

Shipping vehicles via FCL (40ft container) is more secure and cost-efficient than LCL (Less-than-Container Load), especially for higher-value vehicles.

  • Estimated Cost (as of 2025): RMB 20,000 – 50,000 per 40ft container (subject to port and fuel surcharges)

  • Transit Time: Approximately 20–30 days from major Chinese ports (e.g., Ningbo, Shanghai, Tianjin) to Jeddah or Dammam in Saudi Arabia

For new energy vehicles, we often ship with compatible DC fast chargers, and we support charging port conversions (e.g., from GBT to CCS2) to meet Saudi Arabia’s CCS2 charging standards.

3. Saudi Customs Clearance Procedures

Upon arrival at Jeddah or Dammam port, the importer must complete the following:

Required Import Documentation

  • Original or Telex Release Bill of Lading

  • SASO-COC Certificate

  • Commercial Invoice (including CIF value)

  • Packing List

  • Certificate of Origin (COO)

  • Arabic Labeling: The vehicle nameplate must include both Arabic and English, with clear VIN and engine number

  • Importer’s CR (Commercial Registration): The Saudi consignee must hold an active commercial license

Tax and Duty Overview

  • Import Duty for Gasoline Vehicles: Ranges from 5% to 20%, depending on engine displacement

  • Electric Vehicles: Currently exempt from import duty

  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 15% of the CIF (Cost + Insurance + Freight) value

Tip: Work closely with a licensed Saudi customs broker to calculate CIF valuation correctly and avoid under-declaration penalties.

Physical Inspection

Saudi Customs reserves the right to perform rigorous inspections. Any discrepancy between physical vehicles and document specs (such as VIN, chassis, trim level, or powertrain) can result in:

  • Immediate detention

  • Delays in clearance

  • Additional demurrage and port storage fees

4. Risk Management and Tips for a Smooth Export Process

Best Practices for Successful Clearance

  • Start SASO-COC and energy efficiency certification processes early (minimum 90 days before shipment)

  • Label all vehicles with dual-language nameplates (Arabic and English)

  • Use trusted inspection agencies approved by SASO, such as TÜV, SGS, or Intertek

  • Partner with experienced freight forwarders and Saudi customs brokers

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Last-minute documentation mismatches (VIN or engine number errors)

  • Shipping NEVs without proper charger compatibility

  • Submitting expired or incomplete SASO certificates

  • Relying on post-shipment documentation correction — not accepted in Saudi clearance
